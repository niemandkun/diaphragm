About me

Hi, everyone! My name is Ivan Poroshin and I'm third year computer science bachelor student and enthusiastic programmer, interested in robotics, computer vision, bioinformatics and more.

Among the internets you can also find me by nickname niemandkun.

I have interests in various fields, but the most passionate I am in:

  • Robotics
  • Bioinformatics
  • Computer vision
  • Machine learning
  • Gamedev and computer graphics
  • Functional programming

My current location is Yekaterinburg. Here I'm studying math and CS as a student of The Institute of Mathematics and Computer Sciences of The Ural Federal University.

Science

I've spent last summer as a volunteer worker in The Laboratory of Mathematical Modelling with Supercomputer Technologies at The Institute of Immunology and Physiology RAS, where I've got a chance to help the researchers in their study of how to cure human heart diseases.

There I've learnt computer vision and have written a lot of Python code using NumPy, SciPy, pandas, scikit-learn, scikit-image and OpenCV.

As a revard I've got a chance to attend The International Conference On Bioinformatics of Genome Regulation and Structure/Systems Biology at Novosibirsk State University as a listener. There I've learnt a lot about biology.

Also, in September 2016 I've attended BRICS Astronomy Workshop as a listener interested in astronomical data processing.

Programming

After the first year of studying I've decided to participate with my mates in local competitions on virtual robotics, where participants were asked to write a program for a fully autonomous robot acting in a virtual environment.

Our team was lucky enough to take the first place with the solution that used only some graph algorithms and a finite-state automaton.

Now I'm taking part in the development of new competitions, which involves development and implementation of the contest's rules, development of the competitions Unity3D app and the infrastructure maintenance.

As a first years student I wrote a bunch of code in C#, Java and Python. I'm still loving these programming languages and still using them in my daily work.

Furthermore, I've became familiar with Prolog, Haskell and Scala and have fallen in love with the functional programming. Now I'm solving Rosalind problems using Haskell.

As a hobby I'm doing various gamedev, web or assembly stuff. Some of that stuff is available on my projects page.

Besides this I like to play games and have a Steam profile. So, if you'd like to play together, just let me know.

Also, I have a Rosalind profile.
